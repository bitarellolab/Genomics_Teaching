Books

All are available either as hard copy or online format via TRIPOD:

Textbook and other reference books

  • “Genomics: A Very Short Introduction”, Archibald, J. (2018) – as the name suggests, a very good (and short) overview of the field.
  • “Phylogenomics: a primer”, DeSalle, R., Tesla, M., Rosenfeld, J. (2021) - good textbook on basics of phylogenomics
  • “An introduction to molecular anthropology”, Stoneking, M. (2017) - good textbook on biological/molecular anthropology
  • “Phylogenomics: an introduction” ( Bleidorn, C (2017) – phylogenomics textbook
  • “The Princeton guide to evolution”, several authors. (2014 )- excellent reference book
  • “Introduction to Genomics”, Arthur Lesk - genomics textbook. Both 2nd and 3rd editions are good. Physical copies available at Collier.

Other books (mostly/somewhat) about genomics

  • “A Crack In Creation: Gene Editing and the Unthinkable Power to Control Evolution”, Jennifer Doudna & Samuel Sternberg. (2017)
  • “A Brief History of Everyone Who Ever Lived”, Adam Rutherford. (2017) - ancient DNA (Neanderthals, human evolution and dispersal around the world, etc.)
  • “Life’s Edge: the search for what it means to be alive”, Carl Zimmer. (2021) - my favorite science writer. This is about “What is life” but it has some fantastic material explaining how sequencing technologies were developed.
  • “Neanderthal man: In search of lost genomes”, Svante Päabo. (2013) - ancient DNA (Neanderthals, human evolution and dispersal around the world, etc.)
  • “Symbiotic theory”, Lynn Margulis. - this is not about genomics, but it describes Margulis’ endosymbiotic theory for the origin of mitochondria and chloroplasts.
